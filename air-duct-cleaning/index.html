NADCA Guidelines

The Science of Source Removal

At Empire Air Ducts Cleaning we adhere strictly to the National Air Duct Cleaners Association standard known as Source Removal. This is the only scientifically proven method to safely and permanently remove contaminants from an HVAC system without pushing them back into your breathable airspace. Over time duct systems in Riverside County gather extreme amounts of debris, making this intense process absolutely necessary.

Step 1 System Isolation and Negative Pressure
We begin by sealing off your supply and return registers. We then attach a massive commercial grade vacuum unit equipped with hospital grade HEPA filters directly to your main HVAC trunk line. This places the entire system under intense negative pressure creating a powerful one way airflow out of your house.

Step 2 Pneumatic Agitation
While the system is under this intense suction we introduce specialized pneumatic tools such as compressed air whips and rotary brushes deep into the ductwork. These tools safely but aggressively scrub the interior walls of your flex and rigid metal ducts.

Step 3 Safe Eradication
As decades of caked on dust, dead skin cells, post construction debris, and pet dander are dislodged by the whips, the negative pressure instantly pulls the hazardous particulate matter out of your home. Without proper cleaning this material remains trapped in your home forever, constantly recirculating into the air you breathe.

Air Duct Extraction FAQ

Will the negative air pressure vacuum damage my flexible ductwork?

No. Our equipment is specifically calibrated to safely handle all types of residential and commercial ventilation in Murrieta, including standard flexible ducting, rigid sheet metal, and fiberglass duct board. Our pneumatic whips are gentle on the duct walls while remaining highly effective at dislodging debris.

Why should I avoid hiring a budget carpet cleaner to clean my air ducts?

Many budget companies use standard shop vacuums that lack the cubic feet per minute required to place your entire system under true negative pressure. Without proper negative pressure and commercial HEPA filtration, they often just agitate the dust and blow it directly back into your living space.

What is HVAC sanitization and do I actually need it?

HVAC sanitization involves fogging an EPA registered botanical antimicrobial solution through your freshly cleaned ductwork. We highly recommend it if your system has suffered from moisture issues, has a lingering musty odor, or if you are moving into a previously occupied home in Riverside County and want to neutralize existing biological contaminants.

How long does a comprehensive extraction take?

Larger commercial properties will naturally require additional time to ensure every branch line is fully cleared.

How do Santa Ana winds and wildfire ash affect my HVAC system?

The dry heat and high winds in Riverside County blow massive amounts of fine desert dust, pollen, and wildfire ash into the crevices of your home. This debris is quickly pulled into your return vents, clogging the system and severely reducing your indoor air quality until a professional extraction is performed.

What are the warning signs that my Murrieta home needs immediate duct cleaning?

The most common indicators include a constant need to dust your furniture, spiking energy bills, unexplained allergy flare ups, and visible dark debris building up around your vent covers. If you notice a stale musty odor when the air conditioner turns on, it is time to schedule a deep extraction.
