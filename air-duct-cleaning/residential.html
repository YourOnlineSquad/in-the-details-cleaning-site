Indoor Air Pollution

The Hidden Threat Inside Your Walls

Modern homes are built to be incredibly energy efficient. They are tightly sealed to prevent cool air from escaping during the hot Murrieta summers. However, this tight envelope creates an unintended consequence: indoor air pollution.

Because fresh air cannot easily enter a tightly sealed house, your HVAC system continuously recirculates the exact same air up to seven times a day. Over months and years, normal household activities like shedding pets, cooking, tracking in dirt, and dead skin cells create a massive buildup of particulate matter deep within the dark winding corridors of your ventilation system.

This buildup acts as a sponge for volatile organic compounds from cleaning supplies, airborne cooking grease, and microscopic mold spores. A common issue we see in Riverside County is homeowners thinking that changing their 1-inch return filter is enough. Routine vacuuming and filter changes simply cannot reach the contamination hiding in your main trunk lines. Without proper cleaning, this material is constantly pushed back into the air your family breathes. Only professional negative air extraction can reset your home respiratory system to a zero baseline.

Knowledge Base

Homeowner FAQ

Answers to the most common questions we receive from Murrieta homeowners.

How do I prepare my house for residential duct cleaning?

Preparation is minimal. We simply ask that you ensure clear access to all floor, wall, and ceiling vents throughout the home, as well as clear access to your main furnace or air handler unit. Please secure any pets in a separate room, as our technicians will be moving throughout the house with equipment.

Will the cleaning process leave a mess in my house?

Absolutely not. We utilize a closed-loop negative pressure system. All dust and debris are pulled out of your ductwork and directly into our HEPA-filtered extraction unit. We use drop cloths and corner guards to protect your floors and walls, ensuring your home is left cleaner than when we arrived.

Can you clean ducts in an older home with original ductwork?

Yes. Our technicians are trained to assess the condition of older ductwork before cleaning. If your home has fragile or aging ducts, we adjust our pneumatic agitation pressure to safely clean the system without causing tears, breaks, or damage.

How often should I have my air ducts cleaned in Murrieta?

For most homes in Murrieta and Riverside County, we recommend a professional deep clean every 3 to 5 years. If you have multiple shedding pets, severe allergies, or recently completed a dusty home remodeling project, you should schedule an extraction every 1 to 2 years.

Will cleaning my air ducts help my allergies?

Yes. When your ductwork is full of dust mites, pollen, and pet dander, your HVAC system constantly recirculates those irritants into the air you breathe. By safely extracting these contaminants, we remove the source of the problem and provide massive relief for allergy sufferers.

Does duct cleaning improve HVAC efficiency?

Yes. A thick layer of dust coating your evaporator coil and blower motor restricts vital airflow. This forces your air conditioner to work significantly harder and run longer cycles just to cool your home, which spikes your energy bills. Professional cleaning restores that critical airflow.
