The Reality of Commercial Grease Fires

Every day your commercial kitchen operates, it vaporizes pounds of cooking oils, animal fats, and thick grease. Your hood exhaust system is designed to pull this hazardous smoke filled air out of the kitchen. However, as this hot grease vapor travels up the cold metal ductwork toward the roof, it instantly condenses.

A common issue we see in Murrieta is that high volume restaurants running deep fryers, wok stations, or charbroilers accumulate this grease at an astonishing rate. Over time this causes a highly flammable resin like coating to form on every surface. If an unexpected flare up occurs on the cooktop, the flames can easily reach the baffle filters. If those filters and the ductwork behind them are coated in accumulated grease, the fire will instantly travel up the exhaust shaft.

This transforms your ventilation system into a blast furnace, rapidly spreading the fire to the roof and threatening the entire structure. Routine restaurant hood deep cleaning is not merely a cosmetic chore to keep the stainless steel looking shiny for the chef. It is the absolute most critical fire prevention measure your restaurant must undertake to protect your staff, your patrons, and your business investment from total loss. If your entire building is feeling the effects of poor ventilation, we also provide comprehensive commercial air duct cleaning to restore airflow.

The Bare Metal Extraction Protocol

We do not cut corners. Our Riverside County technicians execute a meticulous, top to bottom extraction process that ensures every inch of your system is stripped down to safe bare metal.

1. Kitchen Waterproofing

Before any work begins, we meticulously drape your prep tables, fryers, stoves, and flooring with heavy duty poly plastic. We create a specialized funnel system under the hood canopy to catch all chemical runoff and grease, ensuring zero cross contamination in your cooking area.

2. Chemical Emulsification

We apply industrial strength caustic degreasers to the baffle filters, the canopy interior, the vertical ductwork, and the rooftop upblast fan. This active chemical soak breaks down the molecular bonds of rock hard baked on carbon and grease.

3. Extreme Heat Pressure Washing

Using specialized hot water pressure washers reaching temperatures over 200 degrees, we blast away the emulsified grease from the roof fan all the way down to the canopy. The boiling water strips the system entirely clean and restores it to safe bare metal.

Restaurant Owner FAQ

Critical safety and compliance answers for Riverside County restaurant owners.

How long does a deep restaurant hood cleaning take?

For a standard commercial kitchen with a 10 to 15-foot canopy, the deep cleaning process takes approximately 3 to 4 hours. Larger kitchens with multiple fryers, charbroilers, or extensive vertical ductwork reaching to the roof will require additional time to ensure a complete bare-metal clean.

Will the hot pressure washing damage my kitchen equipment?

No. Before any water or chemical is sprayed, our technicians meticulously wrap your fryers, stovetops, ovens, and prep tables in heavy-duty commercial plastic. We create a watertight funnel system directly under the hood that safely channels all the grease and wastewater into our designated collection containers.

Why is my restaurant still failing inspections after a cleaning?

If you are failing inspections, it means your previous cleaning company likely only polished the visible canopy and ignored the dangerous vertical ductwork and rooftop upblast fan. Fire marshals inspect the entire system. Empire Air Ducts guarantees a complete, compliant bare-metal clean from the cooktop to the roof.

Do I have to shut down my restaurant for a hood cleaning?

No. We know that closing your doors costs you money. We offer flexible, after-hours scheduling in Murrieta. Our crews arrive after your final dinner service or early in the morning before prep begins, ensuring zero disruption to your daily operations.

Why do charbroilers and wok stations need more frequent cleaning?

High-heat cooking methods like charbroiling and wok frying produce massive amounts of dense grease vapor. This vapor cools rapidly in the ductwork, forming rock-hard carbon deposits much faster than standard ovens. Local fire marshals require higher frequency cleanings for these stations to prevent catastrophic flare-ups.

Do you provide an official NFPA 96 compliance sticker?

Yes. As soon as the bare-metal cleaning is completed, our certified technicians affix an official, dated compliance sticker directly to your hood canopy. This provides immediate proof of maintenance for your local health inspector and fire marshal.
